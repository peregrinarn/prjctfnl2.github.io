A DIFFICULT DAY





One of the hardest days of my life was in high school. This story is about a day when I felt very sad for not having participated in a tournament that was important to me. I studied at the Colegio de Bachilleres, Cancún Dos, where I enrolled in the volleyball paraschool. Some time later, the coach invited us to participate in selective training and I accepted. I started training on the volleyball team with the determination to be one of the best, even if it required a lot of effort. In my last semester, the coach announced that he would select the 12 best players for the inter bachilleres tournament, in which we competed against other teams in the region. I was overjoyed to see my name on the list and trained harder, even taking extra classes at a small club to improve. On the day of the tournament, I was one of the first to arrive, but despite being on the list, the coach never gave me the opportunity to play. When I returned home, I cried all night because the coach had destroyed all my dreams.
